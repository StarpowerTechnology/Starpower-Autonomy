Content: ##### About NASA Science Data. ##### Science Data Licenses. ### Spotlight on Data: NASA Exoplanet Archive. # Spotlight on Data: NASA Exoplanet Archive. Astronomer-vetted data about all these planets are available for anyone to access in the NASA Exoplanet Archive. The NASA Exoplanet Archive is a collaboration between the NASA Exoplanet Science Institute, NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Southern California, and the California Institute of Technology’s IPAC (formerly the Infrared Processing and Analysis Center). The archive’s data come from NASA exoplanet satellites such as Kepler and TESS (Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite) and from ground-based observatories such as KELT (Kilodegree Extremely Little Telescope) and SuperWASP (Wide-Angle Search for Planets). Open exoplanet data, such as the data hosted on the NASA Exoplanet Archive, have enabled several citizen science projects. To learn more about the future of exoplanet citizen science, read the recent feature: How NASA Citizen Science Fuels Future Exoplanet Research. ### NASA Science Data Portal. The Science Data Portal provides a comprehensive list of NASA Science data repositories to increase data discovery and accessibility.
